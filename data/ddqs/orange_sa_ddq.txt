SECTION 1 — COMPANY OVERVIEW
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Orange S.A. (formerly France Télécom S.A.) is the parent company and ultimate
holding entity of the Orange Group, one of the world's largest telecommunications
operators. It is incorporated in France under French company law (société anonyme),
registered at the Paris Trade and Companies Register under SIREN 380 129 866.

Orange S.A. is listed on Euronext Paris (ticker: ORA) and is subject to French
corporate income tax at the standard rate (25% from 2022). It files consolidated
and standalone accounts under IFRS. The French State holds a 22.95% stake via
APE (Agence des participations de l'État); no single private shareholder holds
more than 10%.

Orange S.A. holds IP assets, signs master licence agreements with operating
subsidiaries, and acts as the central funding and governance entity of the Group.
It is not a pure holding company — it has direct employees, operates its own
French telecommunications network, and runs the Orange Labs R&D division.

SECTION 4 — MANAGEMENT INDEPENDENCE AND DECISION-MAKING
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Orange S.A. exercises autonomous decision-making over all material IP matters:

a) Royalty rates: set annually by the Transfer Pricing team (headed by the Group
   Chief Financial Officer) using the Comparable Uncontrolled Transaction (CUT)
   method and benchmarked against publicly available external licence agreements.
   No shareholder approval is required.

b) Licence terms: the Group IP Legal team (25 dedicated lawyers, based in Paris)
   drafts, negotiates, and executes all master licence agreements with subsidiaries.
   No external party has veto rights over licence terms.

c) IP strategy decisions: approved by the Board of Directors annually as part of
   the Group Strategic Plan. The French State (22.95% shareholder) does not hold
   a golden share or special voting right over IP strategy decisions.

d) Dividend policy: determined exclusively by the Board of Directors based on Group
   profitability and balance-sheet strength. There is no obligation, contractual
   or factual, to distribute royalty income received from subsidiaries.

Orange S.A. has confirmed in writing (letter dated 15 November 2025 from the CFO)
that it has no back-to-back royalty arrangement with any entity above it in the
corporate structure, and that royalty income is retained at Orange S.A. level for
R&D reinvestment and general corporate purposes.

SECTION 7 — DEMPE FUNCTION ANALYSIS — BRAND AND TECHNOLOGY IP
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Development:
Orange S.A. (through Orange Labs) directs all R&D strategy for the Orange Group.
The Orange Labs Executive Committee, reporting to the Orange S.A. CEO, approves
all R&D budgets, determines research priorities (5G, AI, cybersecurity, cloud),
and allocates research headcount. Operating subsidiaries may submit R&D requests
but do not independently control R&D investment. Key decisions (investment approval
above €5 million) require Senior Management Committee sign-off.

Enhancement:
Brand enhancement decisions — visual identity evolution, global advertising
strategy, digital customer experience standards — are made by the Orange S.A.
Global Marketing Division. All subsidiary marketing campaigns must comply with
the Orange Brand Manual (revised 2024) issued by Orange S.A. Subsidiaries can
run local tactical campaigns within the brand guidelines but cannot modify core
brand elements (logo, colour palette, brand promise).

Technology platform enhancements are developed centrally by Orange Labs and
rolled out to subsidiaries under the Group Technology Roadmap. Subsidiaries do
not independently develop or modify core platform technology.

Maintenance:
All Orange trademark registrations are maintained by the Orange S.A. IP team.
Annual trademark renewal costs are borne directly by Orange S.A. Patent
maintenance fees are paid from the Orange S.A. IP budget. Subsidiaries are not
responsible for any IP maintenance obligations.

Protection:
The Orange S.A. Group IP Legal team manages all trademark enforcement globally.
Orange S.A. is the named plaintiff in all trademark infringement and counterfeiting
cases. Subsidiaries are required to notify Orange S.A. of any IP infringement
they observe but cannot independently settle or initiate litigation. In 2025,
Orange S.A. spent approximately €8 million on IP enforcement.

Exploitation:
Orange S.A. signs individual Master Licence Agreements (MLAs) with each operating
subsidiary. The MLA for Orange Polska S.A. was signed on 1 March 2000 and has
been renewed annually since. Under the MLA, Orange Polska S.A. pays a royalty
for use of the Orange brand and technology platform. The royalty rate is set by
the Orange S.A. Transfer Pricing team using the CUT method and is benchmarked
annually. The current rate applicable to Orange Polska S.A. is [REDACTED — TP
documentation on file]. Orange S.A. determines all licence terms; Orange Polska
S.A. has no right to sub-license the Orange IP to third parties.

SECTION 8 — PASS-THROUGH OBLIGATION ASSESSMENT
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Contractual pass-through: None. Orange S.A. has confirmed that:
(a) No agreement requires it to forward royalty income from subsidiaries to any
    third party or upstream entity.
(b) There is no back-to-back royalty arrangement between Orange S.A. and any
    entity above it in the corporate structure.
(c) The French State shareholder does not receive any distribution automatically
    linked to royalty receipts.

S.A. and other subsidiaries is retained by Orange S.A. for R&D reinvestment,
brand development, and working capital. Annual dividend payments to shareholders
are discretionary and decided annually by the Board based on overall profitability,
not linked to subsidiary royalty receipts.

SECTION 9 — HOLDING PERIOD — ORANGE POLSKA S.A.
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Orange S.A. has held a controlling stake in Orange Polska S.A. (formerly
Telekomunikacja Polska S.A.) since 1998, when the French State-controlled
France Télécom S.A. (the predecessor entity to Orange S.A.) first acquired
shares in the Polish incumbent operator during the privatisation process.

The current Orange S.A. stake of 50.67% of the share capital and voting rights
of Orange Polska S.A. (as registered with the Warsaw Court Register) has been
held continuously since 2013. The stake has been held by Orange S.A. in an
uninterrupted manner for over 25 years, well in excess of the 2-year threshold
required under Art. 21 of the Polish CIT Act (Interest and Royalties Directive
transposition) and Art. 22(4)(3) of the Polish CIT Act (Parent-Subsidiary
Directive transposition).

SECTION 10 — RELATED-PARTY STATUS AND TRANSFER PRICING
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Orange Polska S.A. is a subsidiary of Orange S.A. and qualifies as a related
party within the meaning of Art. 11a(1)(4) of the Polish CIT Act (shareholding
above 25%). All royalty payments are subject to full Polish transfer pricing
documentation requirements (Art. 11k-11t CIT).

Orange S.A. maintains a Master File and Local File for Orange Polska S.A. in
compliance with Polish TP regulations. The royalty benchmarking analysis is
updated annually and stored in the Orange Group TP documentation system.
